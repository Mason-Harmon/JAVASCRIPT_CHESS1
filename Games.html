Karpov vs. Kasparov, World Championship 1985, game 16


This match took place during the 1985 World Chess Championship between Anatoly Karpov on the Soviet Union, and Gary Kasparov of the Soviet union, this match first followed a similar path to the 12th match played, but that quickly changed when Kasparov was able to position his knight deep into Karpov's position causing him to have to remove it with his queen later ending on turn 40 in Kasparov's victory.

Byrne vs. Fischer, New York 1956


This match took place in New York in 1956 between the then 13-year-old and future world champion Bobby Fischer and Donald Byrne.In this match a mistake by Byrne on turn 11 led to Fischer sacraficing his queen for enough material to safely win the game. .

Kasparov vs. Topalov, Wijk aan Zee 1999


This match, known as Kasparov's Immortal(best chess game) took place at the Hoogovens Wijk aan Zee Chess Tournament 1999 between Garry Kasparov and Veselin Topalov, and started with an unual opening from Topalov(e4 into d6) which then spirals to a rook sacrafice from Kasparov on turn 26 leading to a checkmate just 14 turns later.

Morphy vs. Allies, Paris Opera 1858


This match commonly known as the "Opera Game" took place between Paul Morphy and the Duke of Brunswick and Count Isouard, this game is iconic mainly for its ending where Morphy sacrafices his queen for his win, but also contains textbook lessons on development and king safety..
