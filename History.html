600CE

In the early 7th century CE in India, chaturanga is the earliest known form of chess, it had the infantry(pawn), cavalry(knight), elephantry(bishop) and chariotry(rook). Chaturanga was mainly used for tactical purposes.

654CE

The Arabs conquer Persia making way for chess's rapid spread along the silk road, and most importantly to Europe.

900 CE

Older forms of chess make their way to Russia along the Colga-Caspian trade route.

1000CE

Chess makes its way to Europe where it will slowly develop into its modern form.

1375CE

Chess is banned in France

1500CE

Chess reaches its final form, chess theory starts to develop.

1685CE

Chess is no longer just for nobels and starts to become more commonplace.
